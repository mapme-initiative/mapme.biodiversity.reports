There is a lack of detection for dry forest ecosystems which are found e.g. in the Kaa Iya del Gran Chaco. This lack of detections stems from spectral confusion and deciduous phenology; the “brown” signature of dry-season trees is often indistinguishable from bare soil in global baselines. Research indicates GFW algorithms are fundamentally biased toward humid tropical biomes, frequently misclassifying the sparse, clumped canopies of the Gran Chaco as shrubland. Furthermore, GFW often masks out non-humid regions from its primary forest datasets entirely. Nevertheless Kaa Iya del Gran Chaco was somehow saved from major fire events in the last decade which indicates that this area indeed is less threatened.
